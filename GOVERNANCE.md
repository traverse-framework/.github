# Governance

This document defines who owns and who decides in the `traverse-framework` organization. It applies to every repository in the org.

## Ownership Model

The Traverse project uses a **single-owner (BDFL) model**:

- The project owner is **Enrico Piovesan** ([@enricopiovesan](https://github.com/enricopiovesan)), referred to below as "the Owner."
- The Owner holds final decision authority over every repository in the org: what merges, what releases, what the roadmap is, how these governance documents change, and who holds maintainer access.
- The Owner is the "project owner" referenced in [CLA.md](CLA.md) and holds the license rights granted there, including the right to relicense or commercially license the combined work.

## What Maintainership Means (and Doesn't)

Maintainers and contributors may be granted write or triage access to specific repositories to help the project move faster. That access:

- **does not** confer ownership of the project, its trademarks, or its relicensing rights;
- **does not** create any entitlement to future compensation, equity, or consultation in a sale, transfer, or relicensing of the project;
- may be revoked by the Owner at any time.

Contributors always retain copyright in their own contributions, as stated in the CLA.

## Decision Process

- Day-to-day technical decisions follow [constitution.md](constitution.md), [docs/quality-standards.md](docs/quality-standards.md), and each repo's approved specs.
- Material decisions are recorded as ADRs in the affected repo.
- Disagreements are resolved by discussion in issues/PRs; if consensus is not reached, the Owner decides.
- Changes to org-wide governance (this repo) are made by PR and require the Owner's approval.

## Succession and Transfer

The Owner may transfer ownership of the organization, its repositories, its trademarks, and the rights granted under the CLA — in whole or in part — to a successor or acquirer at the Owner's sole discretion. The open source licenses already granted to the public (Apache-2.0, unless a repo states otherwise) survive any such transfer: code released under an open source license stays open source.
